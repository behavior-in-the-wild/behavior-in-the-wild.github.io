Abstract

"Only in actions can you fully recognize the forces operative in social behavior" - Milgram, 1974.
Large language models (LLMs) have become ubiquitous in various applications, but aligning them with societal expectations remains challenging. Current alignment methods rely heavily on human-annotated datasets, which are expensive, difficult to scale, and often biased towards specific demographic subgroups. We introduce a novel approach for LLM alignment using behavioral data from social media advertising platforms. Our approach is based on the maxim in psychology that opinions determine actions, and actions, in turn, reflect opinions. The only tangible aspect of this relationship is the actions themselves, which are observable through social interactions and behaviors. Leveraging this insight, we developed an instruction fine-tuning method using over 33 million samples derived from 1 million advertisements. Our approach significantly outperforms existing alignment techniques, including those trained on expert annotations or opinion surveys, across multiple benchmarks testing for societal and cultural alignment including GlobalOpinionQA, OpinionQA, CultureNLI, and CultureBank. Our findings suggest the potential for using behaviorally-aligned LLMs to complement traditional public opinion polling and forecast shifts in societal attitudes.
